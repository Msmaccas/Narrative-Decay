### Takeaways

* Most existing tools focus on generating answers or reports from recent data.  They lack persistent thesis objects that accumulate supporting and contradicting evidence over time.  NarrativeDecay fills this gap by maintaining a **thesis ledger** and updating each thesis’s state (forming, crowded, contested, decaying, broken or re‑accelerating) as new evidence arrives.
* Several projects (LangAlpha, Investment‑Team, AWS Hedge Fund Assistant) demonstrate hierarchical or role‑based multi‑agent architectures.  NarrativeDecay will adopt a similar approach but specialise agents for source quality assessment, evidence extraction, contradiction detection, price‑context analysis, scepticism and synthesis.
* Articles on multi‑agent pitfalls stress the importance of long‑term memory and consistent data.  NarrativeDecay will address this by keeping a **permanent evidence ledger** and linking each piece of evidence to the corresponding thesis, preventing context rot.
* Competitor products like Domo’s narrative agent or Jenova’s weekly report emphasise summarisation and automation but do not handle the **half‑life** of narratives or mark them as decaying when evidence fragments.  NarrativeDecay’s key innovation is to track narrative decay based on evidence fragmentation, contradiction and price‑action divergence.
